# POUR SOCIAL

## Alcohol Planning & Cost Estimation Guide (Updated)

### Standard Planning Rule: **14 Drinks Per Bottle**

---

## 1) Standard Pour & Bottle Yield (Your Safe Rule)

* Standard cocktail liquor pour: **1.5 oz**
* 750 mL bottle (“a fifth”): **25.36 oz**
* Theoretical yield: **~16.9 drinks**
* **Pour Social planning yield (safe): *14 drinks per bottle***

Why 14?

* spillage, overpours, heavy-hand guests, recipe variance, ice melt dilution mistakes, etc.

---

## 2) Updated Cost-Per-Drink Math

### Formula

* **Cost per drink = Bottle Price ÷ 14**

# 7) Updated Event Examples (2 drinks per guest)

### Key formulas

* **Total drinks = Guests × 2**
* **Bottles needed = ceil(Total drinks ÷ 14)**

| Guests | Total Drinks | Bottles Needed | Liquor Cost Only (@$1.98/drink) | Total Drink Cost (@$2.48/drink) |
| -----: | -----------: | -------------: | ------------------------------: | ------------------------------: |
|     50 |          100 |          **8** |                        **$198** |                        **$248** |
|    100 |          200 |         **15** |                        **$396** |                        **$496** |
|    150 |          300 |         **22** |                        **$594** |                        **$744** |
|    200 |          400 |         **29** |                        **$792** |                        **$992** |

**Interpretation for clients (simple):**
“For 100 guests at ~2 drinks each, expect roughly **15 bottles total** and around **$400 in liquor**, or about **$500 including mixers/garnishes**.”
