POUR SOCIAL
Master Operations Guide

Purpose

This document outlines the standard procedures used by Pour Social to prepare for, execute, and complete event bartending services. The goal is to ensure every event runs smoothly, efficiently, and professionally.

---

Event Workflow Overview

1. Client Inquiry
2. Client Intake Form Completed
3. Quote Provided
4. Contract Signed
5. Deposit Paid
6. Alcohol Shopping List Provided
7. Event Preparation
8. Event Setup
9. Bartending Service
10. Breakdown and Cleanup
11. Final Payment (if applicable)

---

Pre-Event Preparation Timeline

2–3 Weeks Before Event

• Confirm event details with client
• Confirm guest count estimate
• Finalize drink menu
• Confirm alcohol purchase plan
• Confirm venue rules and setup location

1 Week Before Event

• Confirm bartender staffing needs
• Prepare alcohol shopping recommendations
• Confirm alcohol pickup (if applicable)
• Prepare garnish and mixer shopping list

2–3 Days Before Event

• Client pre-orders alcohol if using pickup option
• Pour Social picks up alcohol (if applicable)
• Purchase fruit, garnishes, and mixers
• Prepare syrups or batch ingredients if needed

1 Day Before Event

• Prepare garnish kits
• Prep simple syrups if needed
• Prepare pre-batched cocktails if applicable
• Pack equipment and bar tools

Event Setup Procedure

Arrival

Arrive 60–90 minutes before bar service begins.

Setup Steps

1. Meet event coordinator or host
2. Confirm bar location
3. Unload equipment
4. Set up bar tools and service area
5. Arrange alcohol bottles
6. Prepare garnish station
7. Fill ice wells and chill ingredients

Bar Layout

Left side

Ice and shaker station

Center

Liquor bottles and pour station

Right side

Garnish station and glassware

---

Bartender Service Standards

Professional Presentation

• Clean attire
• Professional appearance
• Friendly and attentive service

Drink Preparation

• Follow standard recipes
• Use jiggers for consistency
• Maintain clean bar workspace

Guest Interaction

• Be welcoming and polite
• Manage guest flow efficiently
• Monitor guest intoxication responsibly

Safety

• Do not serve visibly intoxicated guests
• Follow venue alcohol policies
• Maintain clean and safe work area

---

Pre-Batched Cocktail Service

When serving pre-batched cocktails:

• Keep batches refrigerated before service
• Shake individual servings with ice when required
• Stir batches before serving if separation occurs

---

Alcohol Monitoring

Bartenders should periodically monitor alcohol levels during service.

If alcohol supplies are running low:

• Notify the event host
• Offer alternative drink suggestions if necessary

---

Event Breakdown Procedure

After bar service ends:

1. Stop drink service
2. Clear bar area
3. Secure remaining alcohol for client
4. Dispose of trash
5. Pack equipment and tools
6. Wipe down service surfaces
7. Confirm with host before leaving

---

Post-Event Tasks

After leaving the event:

• Clean bar tools and equipment
• Restock bar supplies
• Review event performance
• Record notes for future improvement
