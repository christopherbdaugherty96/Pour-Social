POUR SOCIAL
Event Setup & Bartender Operations Manual

Purpose

This document outlines the standard procedures for setting up, operating, and closing a Pour Social bar service during private events.

The goal is to maintain consistent service quality, efficiency, and professionalism.

Pre-Event Preparation

48 Hours Before Event

• Confirm event details
• Confirm guest count
• Confirm alcohol availability or pickup
• Review selected drink menu
• Prepare garnish list

24 Hours Before Event

Prepare equipment kit:

Bar Tools
Shakers
Jiggers
Strainers
Bar spoons
Muddlers
Pour spouts
Wine keys
Bottle openers

Bar Supplies
Bar mats
Garnish trays
Cutting boards
Knives
Ice scoops

Garnish Prep
Cut citrus wedges
Prepare mint bundles
Prepare simple syrups

Equipment Checklist

Portable bar (if required)
Ice bins / coolers
Speed rail
Mixers
Garnishes
Napkins / straws / cups

Arrival Timeline

Arrive 60–90 minutes before service.

Setup Steps

1. Set up bar structure and table covers
2. Organize speed rail with primary liquors
3. Place mixers behind bar
4. Prepare garnish station
5. Fill ice bins
6. Arrange glassware or cups
7. Confirm drink menu visibility

Speed Rail Standard Layout

Left → Right

Vodka
Tequila
Rum
Gin
Whiskey

This keeps pours consistent between bartenders.

Service Procedures

During service bartenders should prioritize:

1. Fast service
2. Clean workspace
3. Guest interaction

Drink Priority Order

Highballs
Batch cocktails
Signature cocktails
Complex cocktails

Pre-Batched Drinks

For high volume events, the following may be pre-batched:

Margaritas
Cosmopolitans
Sangria
Rum Punch

This increases service speed significantly.

Responsible Service Guidelines

Bartenders must:

• verify legal drinking age if uncertain
• refuse service to visibly intoxicated guests
• notify event host if issues occur

Closing Procedures

After service ends:

1. Dispose of unused garnishes
2. Pack bar tools
3. Empty ice bins
4. Clean bar surface
5. Return alcohol to host if required
6. Pack equipment

Post Event Review

After each event review:

• drink popularity
• alcohol consumption
• service efficiency
• guest feedback

This helps improve future events.
