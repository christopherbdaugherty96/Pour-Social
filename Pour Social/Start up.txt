POUR SOCIAL
Legal Setup Guide – Michigan

Purpose

This document outlines the legal steps required to start and operate Pour Social as a mobile bartending service in Michigan while remaining compliant with state alcohol regulations.

This guide provides the correct direction, resources, and official links for completing each step.

---

1. Understand the Michigan Alcohol Service Rules

Michigan allows mobile bartending services to operate without a liquor license if alcohol is not sold or supplied by the business.

Pour Social must follow these rules:

Allowed:
• Provide bartending services
• Provide bar equipment
• Provide mixers and garnishes
• Mix and serve alcohol purchased by the client

Not Allowed:
• Sell alcohol
• Purchase alcohol for clients
• Include alcohol in service pricing
• Run cash bars

Official information from the Michigan Liquor Control Commission:

Michigan Liquor Control Commission – Private Event Bartending
https://www.michigan.gov/lara/bureau-list/lcc

Search for:
“Private Event Bartending Services”

---

2. Register Your Business (LLC)

Register Pour Social as a Limited Liability Company (LLC).

Why this matters:

• Protects personal assets
• Allows business banking
• Required for insurance policies
• Allows hiring employees

Where to register:

Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs (LARA)

Business Registration Portal:
https://cofs.lara.state.mi.us

Search for:
“File Articles of Organization”

Typical cost:
$50

Information required:

• Business name (Pour Social LLC)
• Registered agent
• Business address
• Owner information

---

3. Obtain an EIN (Employer Identification Number)

An EIN is the federal tax ID for your business.

Required for:

• Opening a business bank account
• Hiring employees
• Filing business taxes

Apply directly through the IRS:

IRS EIN Application:
https://irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online

Cost:
Free

Application time:
5–10 minutes

---

4. Open a Business Bank Account

Open a dedicated bank account for Pour Social.

This separates personal finances from business operations.

Bring the following:

• LLC registration confirmation
• EIN letter
• Identification

Recommended banks:

• Chase
• Huntington
• Bank of America
• Local credit unions

---

5. Purchase Business Insurance

Insurance is extremely important for bartending businesses.

You should obtain two types:

General Liability Insurance

Covers:

• property damage
• guest injury
• equipment damage

Typical coverage:

$1,000,000 per occurrence
$2,000,000 aggregate

Liquor Liability Insurance

Covers:

• alcohol-related incidents
• overserving claims
• liability related to intoxicated guests

Common insurance providers:

Thimble
https://www.thimble.com

Next Insurance
https://www.nextinsurance.com

Hiscox
https://www.hiscox.com

Estimated cost:

$200 – $600 per year depending on coverage.

---

6. Register for Michigan Sales Tax (If Selling Supplies)

If Pour Social charges for items such as:

• mixers
• garnishes
• supplies

you may need a Michigan sales tax license.

Michigan Treasury – Sales Tax License
https://michigan.gov/taxes

Search for:

“Michigan Sales Tax License Application”

Michigan sales tax rate:

6%

Note:

Service-only charges typically are not taxed.

---

7. Check Local Business License Requirements

Some cities require a local business registration.

For Belleville, contact:

City of Belleville Clerk's Office

City Website
https://www.bellevillemi.org

Phone:
(734) 699-8900

Ask about:

“Home-based business registration or service business license.”

Typical cost:

$25–$100 annually.

---

8. Bartender Certification (Recommended)

Michigan does not legally require bartender certification.

However, certification improves liability protection and professionalism.

Recommended programs:

TIPS Certification
https://www.gettips.com

ServSafe Alcohol Certification
https://www.servsafe.com

Typical cost:

$40–$70 per person.

Benefits:

• insurance companies prefer it
• venues often request it
• improves responsible alcohol service training

---

9. Create Event Contracts

All events should include a written contract.

Contracts should include:

• alcohol policy
• payment terms
• cancellation policy
• liability disclaimers
• guest responsibility clause

Pour Social Alcohol Policy:

Pour Social operates as a mobile bartending service and does not sell alcohol.

Clients may choose one of the following options:

Option 1 — Client Supplies Alcohol

Clients purchase and bring all alcohol to the event.

Option 2 — Client Pre-Orders Alcohol

Clients may pre-order alcohol from a retailer and Pour Social may pick it up 1–3 days before the event provided the order is fully paid by the client.

---

10. Prepare Your Website Legal Notice

Your website should include a clear alcohol policy.

Recommended footer text:

Pour Social provides bartending services and bar equipment for private events.
Clients are responsible for purchasing all alcohol served at their event.

This statement clarifies that the business does not sell alcohol.

---

11. Additional Best Practices

• Always verify guest age when unsure.
• Never serve visibly intoxicated guests.
• Keep written records of event contracts.
• Maintain insurance certificates for venues.
